# Marrakech: The Ochre City

Beyond the food and sights, Marrakech is a city of profound history, vibrant culture, and unspoken rules.

---

## The Story of the Red City

### Foundation and the Almoravids
Founded in 1070 by the Almoravid dynasty (Abu Bakr ibn Umar and Yusuf ibn Tashfin). The city's famous red sandstone walls — giving it the nickname "The Ochre City" / "The Red City" — were built during this period. The word "Morocco" in most European languages derives from "Marrakech."

### A Tale of Two Cities: The Medina vs. Guéliz
- **The Medina:** Historic, walled heart of the city — UNESCO World Heritage site. A labyrinth of narrow passageways, ancient riads, and the legendary Jemaa el-Fnaa square.
- **Guéliz (La Ville Nouvelle):** Built during the French Protectorate era (from 1912). Modern, European-style district with wide boulevards, contemporary art galleries, upscale restaurants, and modern cafes.

### The Golden Age
The city reached incredible grandeur in the 16th century under the Saadian dynasty. They built the Saadian Tombs and the Ben Youssef Madrasa, making Marrakech the jewel of North Africa.

## Smart Hacks for the Local Experience

### 1. The "Stationary Direction" Hack
Never ask a young man walking on the street for directions — fastest way to get a "faux guide" demanding a tip.
**The Hack:** Only ask someone stationary who cannot leave their post: a shopkeeper inside their store, a waiter at a cafe, or a police officer.

### 2. The 50% Rule of Haggling
Start your counter-offer at 30–50% of the initial quoted price. Haggling is theatrical and friendly. Smile, joke, walk away. 9/10 times they call you back with a fair final price.

### 3. The "InDrive/Roby" Taxi Hack
Taxi drivers are notorious for refusing the meter and overcharging tourists.
**The Hack:** Download **inDrive** or **Roby** (local ride-hailing apps). Transparent, pre-agreed price before you get in. No meter negotiation.

### 4. The Riad Retreat
Moroccan architecture is designed to look inward — no matter how chaotic the street outside, the inner courtyard of a Riad is dead silent. Take a mid-day break on your Riad's rooftop with mint tea to recharge.

### 5. The Cash Management Trick
Never pull out a large wad of cash for a small purchase. Vendors often claim they have no change for large bills, forcing you to tip the difference.
**The Hack:** Keep 20–50 MAD notes and coins in an accessible front pocket. Keep your main cash hidden.
