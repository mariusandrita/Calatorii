# Sightseeing in Marrakech: The Local's Guide

Based on advice from local Moroccan (Arabic) and expat (French) forums.

---

## What to See (And Local Hacks)

### 1. Medersa Ben Youssef (مدرسة بن يوسف)
Masterpiece of Islamic architecture — intricate stucco, cedar wood carvings, beautiful zellij tiles.
- **Local Tip:** Go as soon as it opens in the morning for peace and good photos without hundreds of tourists.

### 2. Le Jardin Secret (الحديقة السرية)
While tourists flock to Jardin Majorelle (massive lines), local expats highly recommend Le Jardin Secret. Hidden in the chaotic Medina, it is a beautifully restored traditional Islamic garden offering absolute calm.

### 3. Maison de la Photographie (دار التصوير)
Vintage photographs of Morocco in a beautiful riad.
- **Local Tip:** Don't just go for the photos. The museum has one of the best and most peaceful rooftop terraces in the Medina. Go here for mint tea and panoramic views of the Atlas Mountains.

### 4. Sidi Ghanem Design District (سيدي غانم)
The modern, creative side of Marrakech completely off the beaten tourist path. Showrooms, concept stores, and contemporary artisan workshops in an industrial district.

### 5. The Souks: Bab El Khemis vs. Souk Semmarine
- **The Trap:** Souk Semmarine is the main artery — beautiful but highly commercialised, very expensive.
- **The Local Alternative:** Wander off the main streets to find hidden artisan workshops where things are actually made (and where prices are much lower). For a truly raw, authentic local experience, visit the flea market at **Bab El Khemis** — very few tourists.

## Tourist Traps & Scams to Avoid

### 1. The "Faux Guides" (Fake Guides)
Young men offer unsolicited directions, claiming "the road is closed ahead." Once you arrive somewhere, they aggressively demand a large tip or lead you to their family's shop.
- **How to avoid:** "Non, merci" or "La, shukran" and keep walking. Use offline maps.

### 2. The Taxi "Compteur" Scam
Driver refuses to use the meter, quotes a flat rate 3–5x the actual cost.
- **How to avoid:** Say "Compteur s'il vous plaît" before closing the door. If refused, walk away.

### 3. Jemaa el-Fnaa Animal & Photo Scams
Snake charmers and monkey handlers place an animal on your shoulder without asking, then demand high fees.
- **How to avoid:** Keep your distance. Agree price before raising your camera.

### 4. The Unsolicited Henna Tattoo
Women grab your hand and draw henna without permission. They often use "black henna" (PPD chemical dye) that causes severe allergic reactions and chemical burns.
- **How to avoid:** Keep hands in pockets. Pull away firmly if grabbed. Real henna is always orange-brown, never black.

## Quick Local Survival Tips
- **Haggling:** Aim to pay around 40–50% of the initial asking price. Be polite, smile, walk away.
- **Cash is King:** Many small shops and stalls cannot break large bills.
- **Dress Code:** Cover shoulders and knees in the Medina.
