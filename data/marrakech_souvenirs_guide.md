# What to Buy in Marrakech: A Guide to Souvenirs & Avoiding Scams

Marrakech is a shopper's paradise famous for centuries-old craftsmanship — but also known for tourist traps and counterfeits.

---

## The Best Authentic Souvenirs

### 1. Berber Rugs (Beni Ouarain, Kilim, Boucherouite)
True works of art, woven by hand. Geometric patterns tell stories from the weaver's life.
- **Local Tip:** Look for slight imperfections in the pattern. Perfect symmetry = factory-produced, not handwoven.

### 2. Leather Goods (Babouches, Poufs, Bags)
Marrakech is famous for its tanneries. Traditional leather slippers (Babouches) and leather poufs are iconic.
- **Local Tip:** Smell the leather. High-quality traditionally-tanned leather has an earthy scent. Strong chemicals or ammonia smell = rushed or cheap tanning.

### 3. Amlou (أملو) & Spices
The true hidden gem buy: **Amlou** — a rich, nutritious spread made from toasted almonds, argan oil, and honey. Often called "Moroccan Nutella." Cheap, light to carry, genuinely local. While Ras-el-Hanout is the famous spice blend, amlou is what locals actually eat.

### 4. Metal Lanterns & Ceramics
Intricately carved brass/copper lanterns and beautifully painted ceramics (traditional Safi green or Fes blue designs) make excellent home decor.

## The Biggest Shopping Traps & How to Avoid Them

### 1. The Fake Argan Oil Scam
Most "pure" argan oil sold by street vendors is heavily diluted with cheap vegetable oil.
- **The Test:** Pure cosmetic argan oil absorbs into the skin almost instantly with no greasy, slippery film. Diluted oil sits on top.
- **The Solution:** Only buy from certified women's cooperatives or established pharmacies. If the price seems like a bargain, it is 100% fake.

### 2. The Saffron Scam
Vendors sell dyed corn silk, safflower, or colored synthetic threads to tourists.
- **The Test:** Never buy powdered saffron (too easily faked). True saffron threads have a complex, earthy/metallic aroma. Dropped in warm water, real saffron slowly turns the water golden-yellow. Instant bright red = dyed fake.
- **The Solution:** Look for the official Moroccan guarantee label: **IGP "Safran de Taliouine"**.

### 3. The "Friendly Guide" Commission Trap
A friendly local walks you to a "cooperative" or "trustworthy family shop." They secretly collect a 30–50% commission from the shopkeeper, added to the price you pay.
- **The Solution:** Always find shops independently.

## Where to Shop

### 1. The Souks (For the Experience)
Head into specialized souks: **Souk Smata** (shoes) or **Souk Haddadine** (metalwork).
- **Haggling Rule:** Start at 30–50% of their initial asking price. Never ask the price of an item unless you intend to buy it.

### 2. Ensemble Artisanal (For Price Anchoring)
Government-regulated complex where local artisans sell at **fixed, fair prices**. Visit here first to learn what a fair price looks like before entering the souks.

### 3. Sidi Ghanem (For Modern Design)
High-end contemporary Moroccan design (furniture, fashion, modern ceramics) without the medina chaos.
