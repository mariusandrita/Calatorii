# The Marrakech Bucket List: Experiences, Hacks, & Traps

Beyond sightseeing and eating, Marrakech is about experiencing the culture.

---

## 1. The Hammam (Moroccan Bathhouse)
Going to a Hammam is a cornerstone of Moroccan culture. Black soap (savon noir) and a kessa glove scrub you down until you are cleaner than you've ever been.

### The Two Types:
- **The Local "Quartier" Hammam:** ~15–30 MAD. Very spartan, public, authentic. Bring your own soap, towel, and scrub glove. About hygiene and socialising for locals.
- **The Tourist Spa:** 150–600+ MAD. Private or semi-private, luxurious, includes massages, oils, and English-speaking staff.

### The Hammam Traps:
- **The "Guide" Commission:** Never follow a friendly stranger who recommends a "great hammam nearby." They will collect a massive commission secretly added to your bill.
- **The Naked Upsell:** Some low-quality tourist hammams wait until you are inside (and vulnerable) to aggressively upsell expensive argan oils or extra massages.
- **The Fix:** Book directly via the spa's official website or ask your Riad owner to book a trusted local spot. Confirm the total price *before* undressing.

---

## 2. The Desert Tour
Taking a trip to the Sahara is on everyone's bucket list — but also the source of the biggest tourist scams.

### The Desert Traps:
- **The "Too Cheap" Tour:** If a street agency offers a desert tour for an unbelievably low price, it's a scam. Broken minibus, dirty tent, forced stops at cousin's expensive carpet shops.
- **The "Sahara in a Day" Lie:** True Sahara sand dunes (Erg Chebbi, Merzouga) are a grueling **9–10 hour drive** from Marrakech. Anyone promising "Sahara" in one or two days is either lying or taking you to the Agafay Desert (beautiful, but flat rocky wasteland — not sand dunes).

### The Local Fix:
Book a minimum **3-day/2-night** tour with an established, highly-reviewed local operator *before* you arrive in Morocco — not from a tout in the medina.

## 3. Mint Tea Culture (The Slow Approach)
Mint tea ("Moroccan Whiskey") is a deliberate social ritual, not a quick caffeine fix.

- **The Hack:** Don't drink it at overpriced cafes on the ground floor of Jemaa el-Fnaa. Find a hidden rooftop cafe in the Medina — the higher you go, the quieter it gets. Watching the sunset over the Koutoubia Mosque from a rooftop while sipping sweet mint tea is the ultimate local recharge.

---

## 4. Take a Cooking Class
Local forums highly recommend this as the best way to bridge the gap between tourist and local.

- **The Hack:** Look for a cooking class that explicitly includes a **market visit**. The chef walks you through the local food souks, teaching you how to select vegetables, buy from the butcher, and negotiate for spices before heading back to the kitchen.

---

## 5. Stay in a Riad (Not a Hotel)
Moroccan architecture is historically inward-facing. From the street, a Riad looks like a blank dusty wall with a heavy wooden door. Step inside and it opens into a stunning silent courtyard with fountains, orange trees, and mosaic tiles — a literal sanctuary from the sensory overload outside.
