FIRST NIGHT · THE SQUARE AWAKENS

Medina · Jemaa el-Fna
17:00
Leave Radisson Blu
📍 Hotel lobby, Gueliz
Take a petit taxi (red) to the medina entrance — agree on 20–30 DH before getting in. Or walk 20 min south on Avenue Mohamed VI to Koutoubia.
💡 Say "Jemaa el-Fna, shukran" — every driver knows it.
17:20
Souk des Épices + Souk des Teinturiers
📍 Rahba Kedima → Souk des Teinturiers, Medina
The spice market at golden hour: saffron, ras el hanout, argan oil, dried rose petals. Then duck into the Dyers' Souk — skeins of colored wool hang overhead in the narrow alleys. This is where locals shop, not tourists.
⚠ Ignore men who approach offering to "show you the real souk." Free guides expect money.
18:30
Rooftop sundowner over Jemaa el-Fna
📍 Les Terrasses de l'Alhambra · north side of the square
Watch the square transform from above as the stalls set up below. Fresh orange juice from any vendor = 4 DH (≈ €0.37) — a Marrakech institution. Mint tea with ginseng + cinnamon = 3 DH.
☕ 3–20 DH for drinks
19:30
Walk Jemaa el-Fna as it peaks
📍 Place Jemaa el-Fna · Medina
The square is now alive. Navigate toward the Gnawa musicians — men in blue robes playing the guembri (bass lute) and metal castanets. Their circles form deeper in the square, away from the tourist perimeter. This is authentic Sufi spiritual music, not performance.
Also look for: storytellers (halqa) in Darija, fire eaters, acrobats.
⚠ Monkey + snake handlers: approach only if you agreed a price first. A photo costs 20–50 DH minimum.
20:30
Dinner
📍 Jemaa el-Fna · Medina
OPTION A · LOCAL FAVOURITE

Chez Chegrouni

3 terraces directly overlooking the square. Beloved by locals and long-time visitors. Tajines, couscous, grilled meats, harira soup. Open until midnight.

OPTION B · FULL IMMERSION

Jemaa el-Fna Street Stalls

~50 stalls under white tents. Walk past front hailers to the cheaper back rows. Try: harira, brochettes, merguez, lamb head, snails in cumin broth. Full meal = 10–50 DH.


⚠ At stalls: no menu = negotiated price. Agree before sitting. No printed price = tourist markup risk.
🍽 80–150 DH / person (Chegrouni) · 10–50 DH (stalls)
22:00
Koutoubia Mosque + Mint Tea Ceremony
📍 Koutoubia Mosque · 5 min walk west of the square
The 12th-century minaret is beautifully lit at night — visible from most of Marrakech. Free to admire from outside. Then find a medina café for a full atay ceremony: tea is poured from height to create foam. This is not a quick drink. Sit. Breathe.
🕌 Free (exterior) · ☕ 5–15 DH for tea
23:00+
Return to Gueliz or stay out optional
📍 Hivernage / Gueliz
Taxi back to Radisson, or walk up Boulevard Mohamed VI. The Hivernage neighborhood around the hotel has late bars and lounges if energy allows.

Tuesday, May 19

HAMMAM · PALACE FEAST · LIVE MUSIC

Bab Agnaou · Mellah · Dar Yacout
17:00
Traditional Hammam — Les Bains de Marrakech
📍 2 Derb Sedra, Bab Agnaou · Medina
Book in advance. Classic circuit: steam room → kese scrub (black soap + exfoliation glove) → ghassoul clay mask → massage. Stone floors, real heat, no frills — the authentic version. Closes 19:30 — arrive by 17:00 to get the full treatment.
💡 Bring flip-flops. Wear the hammam wrap they provide. Tip the attendant 20–30 DH.
💆 250–400 DH / person depending on treatment
19:00
Mellah Evening Walk
📍 Mellah (Jewish Quarter) · near Palais el-Badi · Medina
The Mellah is the old Jewish quarter, less visited than the main medina souks. At dusk the covered food market here sells olives, preserved lemons, spices, and fresh produce at real local prices. Just walk and observe. The wrought-iron balconies overhead are characteristic of the Jewish medina architecture — very different from the Moorish arches elsewhere.
20:00
Grand Dinner — Dar Yacout
📍 79 Derb Sidi Ahmed Soussi · Medina · Reserve in advance
A 16th-century palace riad designed by the legendary Bill Willis. The multi-course diffa (ceremonial feast) unfolds over 2+ hours: bastilla (pigeon pie with almonds + cinnamon + powdered sugar), multiple tagines, mechoui (slow-roasted lamb), couscous with seven vegetables, Moroccan pastries and tea. Live oud music on the rooftop terrace under the stars.
This is expensive but it is the genuine grand Moroccan table — not a tourist show, a real palace dinner.
💡 Dress up. This is one of the most beautiful rooms in Marrakech.
🏛 600–900 DH / person · Book days ahead
22:30
Late Evening — Live Music or Rooftop
📍 Palais Jad Mahal or Comptoir Darna
INTIMATE · LIVE MUSIC

Palais Jad Mahal

10 Rue Haroun Errachid. Indo-oriental decor, excellent live group, late bar. Good for cocktails without a loud DJ.

LIVELY · BELLY DANCING

Comptoir Darna

Ave Echouhada. Oriental decor, belly dancers, food and drinks. Opens 20:00, stays until 3:00. More theatrical but genuinely fun.

Thursday, May 21 — Last Night

DEEP MEDINA · FAREWELL GNAWA

Souk el-Kebir · Nomad · Jemaa el-Fna
17:00
Deep Souk Circuit — The Working Medina
📍 Souk el-Kebir → Souk Cherratine → Rahba Kedima · Medina
Go through Souk el-Kebir and veer off the tourist drag into the residential sections: cobblers, carpenters, metalworkers, tailors working in cells no bigger than a wardrobe.

Souk Cherratine — leather workers.
Rahba Kedima — the "witch square" where locals buy medicinal herbs, dried chameleons, kohl, and traditional remedies. Just observe.
💡 If you want to buy anything in the souks: agree the price before picking up the item. The first price is 3–5× the real price. Walk away once and they'll follow with the real number.
18:30
Sundowner at Nomad Rooftop
📍 Derb Berdaa 1, near Rahba Kedima · Medina
Rooftop restaurant with colorful Moroccan textiles, panoramic views over the medina roofscape, and a relaxed modern vibe. Good for fresh juice, cocktail, or a light starter (cumin calamari, cauliflower salad, harissa chicken) as the light fades.
🥂 100–250 DH for drinks and starters
19:30
Farewell Dinner — Three Authentic Choices
📍 Medina
MOST LOCAL · COOPERATIVE

Amal Centre

Social cooperative employing disadvantaged Moroccan women. Home-style cooking: lentil salads, fish tagine, traditional bread. Very cheap, very real — check dinner hours on the day.

MEDINA · LOCAL CROWD

Naranj

Riad Zitoun el Jdid. Lebanese-Moroccan fusion, kilim-decorated space, hand-cut lanterns. Excellent mezze. Strong local crowd. Not a tourist trap.


Or: Jemaa el-Fna street stalls — go back one final time and try what you haven't yet: babbouche (snails in cumin broth), seffa (sweet couscous with raisins + almonds), or msemen flatbread with argan and honey.
🍽 50–200 DH / person depending on choice
21:30
Final Gnawa Circle — The Real Closing
📍 Jemaa el-Fna · deeper into the square
Return to the square after dinner. By 21:30 the tourist perimeter is thinner and the Gnawa circles are deeper and more intense. The guembri bass drone and metal castanet rhythms are trance-inducing. This is the real Marrakech memory to take home.
💡 Stand at a respectful distance. Ask before photographing. Drop 20–30 DH in their hat if you stay long.
23:00+
Theatro Nightclub optional · opens midnight
📍 Rue Ibrahim El Mazini · Hivernage
Entry 300 DH. Opens midnight, runs until 5:00. DJs, live acrobats, dancers, and magicians. Young Marrakchi crowd alongside tourists. If you have energy left — this is the place.
🎭 300 DH entry · drinks extra

⚡ Practical Essentials

Taxi Petit taxi (red), agree 20–30 DH to medina before getting in. Never use the meter.
Cash Most street stalls and hammams are cash only. ATMs in Gueliz. 1 EUR ≈ 10.8 MAD.
Dress Cover shoulders + knees in the medina, near mosques. Bring a layer — evenings are cool.
Language French opens more doors than English. "Shukran" (شكراً = thank you), "La shukran" (no thank you) is essential.
Phones Keep phones in front pockets in the square. Pickpockets work in crowds.
Reservations Dar Yacout: book 2–3 days ahead. Hammam: call same morning. Agafay: book online day before.
Orientation Koutoubia minaret is visible from most of the medina. Always navigate toward it to find your way out.
Negotiating First price is 3–5× the real price. Walk away once. They'll follow with the real number.
Photos Gnawa musicians, food stall workers: drop 20–30 DH in the hat if you photograph. Always ask first.
